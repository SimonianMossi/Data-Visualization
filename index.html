Femicides

Abstract

Homicides in general context are quite rare. But they are the subject of media interest and numerous works of fiction. Usually these seem to focus on cases with unusual forms of homicide such as those with a sexual or unknown motive, as well as those carried out by a serial killer for example. On the contrary, the data shows that the majority of murders are committed by the victim's intimates or by young men in conflicts and fights. The analysis and observation of homicide statistics must be done with a careful approach. Below, visualizations of some homicide data are presented. We need to keep in mind that these data are not necessarily accurate, as each country's legal definitions of homicide may differ, resulting in the collection and recording of homicides being equally different. A typical example is Japan, which does not record homicides in a robbery as homicides.

Femicides

It has been 46 years since Diana Russell coined the word "Femicide" in March 1976 at the 1st international tribunal for crimes against women in Brussels. The aim of this word was to make the world aware that the violent death of women is a crime that should not be confused with the gender-neutral term "Homicide". Characteristically, she had stated that 'it is impossible to mobilize against something without a name, that is, something that until now has been "invisible". Words are what give meaning to the world.
Despite the fact that men are murdered more often than women, there are very few cases where this happens because of their gender. Femicide occurs in patriarchal societies, in which women are expected to be receptive and passive. So femicide, to give the definition, is the killing of a woman or girl mainly by a man (usually a partner) because of her gender alone. Elements such as patriarchal values can increase the risk of violence due to the belief that women can and should be controlled by men. They are exposed to all forms of violence and pressure. The right to life is violated. Its main characteristic is that it is systematic, continuous and conscious. Women face violence and are exposed to femicide around the world from childhood.

Conclusion

As mentioned before for the homicide records, calculating the exact number of femicides is challenging. The data are not collected from all countries, nor are they sent to the UNODC international database entitled "women intentionally killed because of their gender by their partners".. Data depending on the country, agencies and competent authorities can be collected with different criteria. Additionally, they are not collected by all countries annually, creating enough gaps (for the statistics) to be easily compared. Apart from cases that are not recorded due to ignorance, there are cases that are wrongly recorded as deaths due to health issues or accident.. Cases of femicide in the context of human trafficking, forced prostitution and female genital mutilation fall under the category of femicide. In addition, another incorrect register is the case in which a family that cannot provide food for all the children, neglects the girls resulting in malnutrition and ultimately their death. Finally, the covid-19 pandemic has been a huge obstacle not only to data collection, but first and foremost an obstacle for many women to find safe shelter from violent partners and to access hospital facilities and services. To conclude, we reach 2 important “verdicts”.
1) By informing the world about femicides the purpose is both to raise public awareness and potential victims to seek help and support. This work requires the collective participation of professionals such as teachers, health workers, members of the police force, judicial authorities, officials who know how to handle cases of gender-based violence, as well as women themselves in associations and communities.
2) Femicide/feminicide whenever and wherever it occurs, it is vital that it be called by its name in the media and not by terms that have been heard much in the past such as "crime of passion" so that the seriousness of the crime is being felt by the public.
